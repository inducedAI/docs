<Note>
  This guide assumes you have access to the Outlook account from which you want to forward emails and the email address to which you want to forward them.
</Note>

## Overview

Email forwarding in Outlook enables you to automatically forward incoming emails to another email address. This feature is useful for managing multiple email accounts or ensuring you receive important messages in a single inbox.

## Steps to Set Up Email Forwarding

### Step 1: Access Outlook Settings

1. Open [Outlook](https://outlook.live.com/) and log in to your account.
2. Click the gear icon in the top-right corner to open the Settings menu.
3. Select **View all Outlook settings** at the bottom of the menu.

### Step 2: Navigate to the Forwarding Settings

1. In the Outlook settings window, go to the **Mail** category.
2. Click **Forwarding** in the left sidebar.

### Step 3: Enable Forwarding

1. Check the **Enable forwarding** checkbox.
2. Enter the email address to which you want to forward your emails in the **Forward my email to** field.
3. Optionally, you can keep a copy of forwarded messages in your Outlook inbox by checking the **Keep a copy of forwarded messages** checkbox.
4. Click **Save** at the top of the page to apply your changes.

### Step 4: Set Up Selective Forwarding (Optional)

1. To forward only specific emails, go back to the main settings window and select **Rules** under the **Mail** category.
2. Click **Add new rule**.
3. Set your criteria for forwarding, such as emails from a particular sender or with a specific subject line.
4. In the actions section, choose **Forward to** and enter your forwarding address.
5. Name your rule and click **Save** to activate selective forwarding.

## Conclusion

You have successfully set up email forwarding in Outlook. All incoming emails will now be automatically forwarded to the specified email address. You can return to the settings menu at any time to modify or disable email forwarding.

<Tip>
  Regularly check the inbox of your forwarding address to ensure that emails are being forwarded correctly and to confirm that no important messages are being missed.
</Tip>
